PLUGIN_UPNPBRIDGE_CODECSPARAM
	EN	Audio codec parameters

PLUGIN_UPNPBRIDGE_CODECSPARAM_DESC
	EN	The bridge can either simply passthrough audio data to the player (with minor alteration) or do a full transcoding which
	EN	improves compatibility by sending controlled format and allows replay gain as well as fading in and out. It's also 
	EN	possible to send audio in a continuous flow of data instead of tracks one-bye-one to enable true gapless and 
	EN	cross-fading. See user's guide for more details
	EN	<br>- <i>Mode:</i> set processing mode. Format is [thru|pcm|flac:[q]][,r:[-]<rate>][,s:8|16|24][,flow]. "thru" is simple 
	EN	passthru, "pcm" and "flac" are for decoding, re-encoding and sending in "pcm" or "flac" to player. When re-encoding
	EN	"r:" forces	a maximum sample rate when predicated with "-" or a fixed sample rate if not. "s:" is to set sample size. 
	EN	"flow" forces creates a continuous sample flow of audio, enabling true gapless and cross-fading, but metadata will not 
	EN	be sent in that mode. 
	EN	<br><i>- Codecs:</i>  reports supported codecs to LMS. Comma-separated, list made of mp3, flc, aif, pcm, aac, ogg
	EN	and alc. In passthru mode, if a listed codec is NOT supported by the UPnP player, it will be ignored while if re-encode
	EN	is enabled, local decoding will be done by the bridge (only mp3,aac,flac,pmc and ogg are supported)
	EN	<br>- <i>PCM format:</i> comma-separated list (<b>ordered</b>) of supported uncompressed audio formats (amongst 
	EN	wav, aiff, raw). These formats are used when sending pcm audio to the UPnP Player.
	EN	<br><i>- Max rate:</i>  reports maximum sample rate to LMS. This does not influence local resampling, it's just an 
	EN	information for LMS to decide to do transcoding.
